About

Who am I

I hold a dual bachelor’s degree in Telecommunications Systems Engineering and Computer Engineering from the Autonomous University of Barcelona (UAB). After a year at the Polytechnic University of Catalonia (UPC) completing an MSc in Telecommunications Engineering with a focus on AI, I’m moving to Sweden to pursue a double master’s degree in Machine Learning at KTH.

I started out doing research at UOC’s Wireless Networks group (WiNe), working on antenna-based sensors, electromagnetic characterization of materials, and passive RFID systems. I’m now a Machine Learning Research Intern at the Barcelona Supercomputing Center, working on graph neural networks for spatiotemporal interpolation and forecasting of Earth system variables.

Outside of research and studies — which doesn’t leave much free time — I like to play chess and poker, both of which I’ve used as an excuse to think more seriously about strategy and decision-making under uncertainty.
